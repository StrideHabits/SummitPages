Privacy & Data Protection

Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how Stride Habits collects, uses, and protects your information when you use the app to track and complete habits.

App: Stride Habits
Platform: Android (Google Play)
Scope: Use of the Stride Habits mobile application
Last updated: 16 November 2025
01

Introduction

Stride Habits is a personal habit app that allows you to create habits, schedule them, track completions and streaks, and optionally attach images to your entries. By installing or using Stride Habits (the “App”), you agree to the collection and use of information as described in this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree with this Policy, please uninstall the App and discontinue use.

02

Information We Collect

The App is designed to collect only the information needed for habit tracking and basic diagnostics. This includes:

  • Habit data – habit names, schedules, categories, notes, completion history, and streaks.
  • Reminder data – times and settings for notifications and reminders you enable for specific habits (where available).
  • Images you attach – photos or images you choose to link to a habit or completion entry. These are stored locally on your device and are not uploaded to the Summit API or other remote storage.
  • Device and usage data – anonymous technical information such as device model, operating system version, app version, and basic in-app events used to keep the App stable and improve features.

The App does not require you to create a public profile or share your habit content with other users.

03

How Your Information Is Stored

Stride Habits uses a combination of local storage on your device and a small backend service called the Summit API.

  • On your device – your habits, completions, settings, and any images you attach are stored in a local database and files on your device.
  • Summit API (backend) – text-based habit data (such as names, schedules, and completion flags) may be synchronised to the Summit API when your device is online so that the App can keep data consistent between sessions and devices while this project is active.

Images are currently stored locally only and are not sent to the Summit API or to Firebase storage.

Summit API exists to support a university (varsity) software project. It has no commercial business model and no long-term plan to keep data beyond the life of the project. When the project is shut down, data stored in the Summit API will be deleted as part of that shutdown, subject to any short-term backups retained by the hosting provider.

04

How We Use Your Information

Information collected by the App is used to:

  • Display and manage your habits, streaks, and completion history.
  • Schedule and deliver reminders or notifications you choose to enable.
  • Store your in-app preferences (such as theme or language, where available).
  • Maintain and improve app stability, performance, and security.

Habit content (names, notes, and attached images) is used only to provide the features inside Stride Habits and is not used for advertising profiling or sold to third parties.

05

Third-Party Services

The App may rely on the following platform services and libraries:

  • Google Play and Google Play Services – for installation, updates, and platform-level services.
  • Firebase Authentication – to support optional Google Sign-In (Google SSO) so that you can log in with your Google account instead of a separate password.
  • Firebase Crashlytics and related diagnostics – to process anonymised crash reports and technical events so problems can be identified and fixed.
  • Hosting provider for Summit API – to run the backend server that temporarily stores habit data while the project is active.

These services may receive limited technical information (for example, anonymous identifiers, crash logs, device type, and app version) in order to operate. They process that information under their own privacy policies. Stride Habits does not include personalised advertising SDKs and does not sell your data.

06

Data Sharing

Stride Habits is not a social network and does not automatically share your habit data with other users.

Data may be shared only in limited circumstances, for example:

  • With service providers that host or process data on our behalf (such as the Summit API hosting provider and Firebase), under terms that limit their use of the data to operating and improving the service.
  • When required by law, regulation, or a valid legal request.

Your data is not sold to third parties and there is no intention to commercialise user data from this project.

07

Data Security

Reasonable technical and organisational measures are used to help protect your information against loss, misuse, and unauthorised access. However, no method of storage or transmission is completely secure, and complete security cannot be guaranteed.

Where possible, communication with third-party services uses encrypted connections (such as HTTPS/TLS), and the App limits collection to what is necessary to provide its features.

08

Your Rights & Controls

Depending on your country or region, you may have specific rights over your personal data (for example, to access, correct, or delete it). Within the App and on your device, you generally have the ability to:

  • Edit or delete habits, notes, and completion entries directly in the App.
  • Delete attached images by removing them from the relevant habit or entry.
  • Disable notifications for specific habits or for the App entirely through system settings.
  • Clear the App’s local data or uninstall the App, which normally removes locally stored data from your device.

If the App later provides additional ways to export or manage your data (for example, an export to file feature), those options will be described in-app.

09

Retention

Your habit data is retained on your device for as long as you keep it in the App and do not delete it.

Data stored in the Summit API exists only to support this varsity project. It is intended to be kept for the duration of the project and then removed when the project is shut down. Short-term backups may be retained for a limited period by the hosting provider but are not kept indefinitely.

10

Children’s Privacy

Stride Habits is designed as a general-audience productivity tool for adults and older teens and is not aimed at young children. It does not knowingly collect personal information from children under the minimum age of digital consent in your region without the involvement of a parent or legal guardian.

If you are a parent or guardian and believe that a child has provided personal data through the App without your consent, you can remove that data by deleting the relevant habits and entries or by uninstalling the App from the child’s device.

11

Not Medical or Professional Advice

Stride Habits is a simple habit and routine tracker intended to support general wellbeing and productivity. It is not a medical, mental-health, or professional healthcare application and does not provide medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment.

You use the App at your own risk. Do not rely on Stride Habits for decisions about your physical or mental health. Always consult a qualified professional for medical or psychological concerns.

Changes to This Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated from time to time, for example to reflect new features or changes in legal requirements. When updates are made, the “Last updated” date at the top of this Policy will be changed.

If a change is significant, reasonable steps will be taken to draw your attention to it (for example, highlighting it in the App or on the store listing) where required. Continued use of the App after changes become effective means you accept the updated Policy.

13

Questions and Contact
