Stride Habits – Help & FAQ

Answers to common questions about using Stride Habits, a simple habit tracker built as a varsity project.

App: Stride Habits
Platform: Android (Google Play)
Focus: Getting started, features, and troubleshooting
Last updated: 16 November 2025
01

Getting Started

What is Stride Habits?

Stride Habits is a light-weight habit app where you can create routines, track completions and streaks, and optionally attach images to your entries. The goal is to give a clean, focused place to build small, positive habits.

Do I need an account?

You can use Stride Habits with basic local storage only. Some builds may offer Google Sign-In (via Firebase Authentication) to sync certain text-based habit data through the Summit API while the project is active. If you prefer to stay purely offline, you can choose not to sign in.

02

Adding, Editing, and Deleting Habits

How do I add a new habit?

  • Open the main Habits screen.
  • Tap the add / plus button.
  • Enter a habit name, choose how often it should repeat, and adjust any options (reminders, category, etc.).
  • Save the habit to start tracking it.

How do I edit a habit?

  • Tap on the habit you want to change.
  • Use the edit option (usually an icon or menu item).
  • Update the schedule, name, or other fields and save.

How do I delete a habit?

  • Open the habit details or long-press on the habit.
  • Select the delete option and confirm.
  • Deleting a habit may also remove its completion history from your device.
03

Tracking Completions & Streaks

How do I mark a habit as done?

  • From the main list or calendar view, tap the day or checkmark for a habit.
  • The app records that completion and updates your streak.

How are streaks calculated?

Streaks are based on how many scheduled days in a row you complete the habit. Skipping a scheduled day usually breaks the streak. Non-scheduled days do not affect the streak.

04

Images & Attachments

Can I attach photos to habits?

Yes. You can attach images to certain habit entries (for example, a photo of a workout or completed task). This is optional.

Where are images stored?

Images are stored locally on your device only. They are not uploaded to the Summit API or any cloud storage as part of this project. If you uninstall the app or clear its data, these attachments are normally deleted with it.

05

Reminders & Notifications

How do I turn reminders on?

  • Edit a habit and enable reminders for that habit.
  • Choose the time(s) when you want to be reminded.
  • Make sure notifications for Stride Habits are allowed in your Android system settings.

I am not receiving notifications.

  • Check that notifications for the app are enabled in system settings.
  • Confirm that battery or power-saving modes are not blocking background work for Stride Habits.
  • Verify that the reminder time has not already passed for that day.
06

Accounts, Sync & Summit API

What is the Summit API?

Summit API is a small backend service used for this varsity project to temporarily sync certain text-based habit data (like habit titles and completion flags) across sessions or devices. It is not a commercial product and has no long-term data retention plan.

Is everything synced?

  • Text-based habit information may be synced to Summit API while the project is live.
  • Images and attachments remain on your device and are not uploaded to Summit API.

What happens when the project ends?

When the university project is shut down, the Summit API and its stored data are planned to be removed, subject to short-lived backups from the hosting provider. Stride Habits is not designed or marketed as a permanent cloud backup solution.

07

Privacy & Security Basics

Which third-party services are used?

Stride Habits may use:

  • Google Play Services (distribution and platform support).
  • Firebase Authentication for optional Google Sign-In.
  • Firebase Crashlytics and diagnostics for crash and error reporting.
  • A hosting provider for the Summit API backend.

There are no personalised advertising SDKs inside Stride Habits, and data is not sold to third parties.

For full details, refer to the dedicated Privacy Policy page if hosted, or the privacy link in the Play Store listing.

08

Troubleshooting & Known Limitations

The app feels slow or crashes.

  • Make sure you are running the latest version of Stride Habits from Google Play.
  • Restart the app or, if needed, restart your device.
  • If the problem repeats, Crashlytics may send an anonymised report to help the developer identify the issue in future updates.

Will there be constant updates?

Stride Habits is primarily a varsity project, created for learning and assessment. It may not receive regular long-term updates and is provided on an “as is” basis.

What should I do before uninstalling?

  • If you want to keep your habit history or images, manually back them up before uninstalling (for example, by copying screenshots or your own notes).
  • Uninstalling the app or clearing its data normally removes all locally stored habit data and image attachments.

Health & Safety Disclaimer

Stride Habits is a “good vibes” habit tracker designed to encourage routines such as reading, exercise, or productivity tasks. It is not a medical, mental-health, or professional treatment tool.

Do not rely on Stride Habits for medical, psychological, or emergency decisions. Always consult qualified professionals for health and safety matters.
